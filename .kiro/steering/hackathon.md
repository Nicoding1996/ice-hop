# Hackathon Constraints & Strategy

Reddit "Games with a Hook" hackathon. Submission window June 17 - July 15, 2026
(6pm PT). Judging through July 27; winners ~July 29. $40k total prizes.

## Judging criteria (equally weighted)

1. Delightful UX - instantly fun, fits in the viewport, no instructions needed,
   mobile-first.
2. Polish - launch-ready, no broken states, understandable from the demo post.
3. Reddity - community-first, NOT Reddit-themed. The comment section should be
   part of the gameplay. Do not build around karma/Snoo/subreddits as a gimmick.
4. Hook - a concrete reason to return day after day.
5. Phaser Innovation - only for the Phaser category.

## Prizes to target

- Best App with a Hook - $15k (stretch)
- Best Use of Phaser - $5k (if Phaser elevates gameplay)
- Best Use of Retention Mechanics - $3k (PRIMARY)
- Best Use of User Contributions - $3k (PRIMARY)
- Honorable Mention - $1k x10 (realistic)

## What we are NOT doing (their explicit "no" list)

- AI slop: generic look, doesn't fit the screen, no identity. Counter with a
  distinct visual identity and tight polish.
- On-the-nose Reddit theming (karma/Snoo as the point).
- Literal "hook" (fishing). Hook = retention.
- Common ideas: trivia, clones of popular games, simple platformers, space
  shooters, generic collaborative storytelling. Our differentiator (solver-
  validated UGC + a playable community puzzle stream, on top of a fresh daily)
  is what lifts us out of "clone".

## IP / identity guardrail (important)

The mechanic is inspired by SmartGames' "Jump In'" but is a public game GENRE
(peg-solitaire / Rush Hour-style), which is not protectable. What IS protected:
that product's NAME, ARTWORK, and its specific PUZZLE SET. Therefore:
- Use our own name, our own art, and our own (generated/UGC) puzzles.
- Keep engine pieces abstract (HOPPER/SLIDER/BLOCKER); the theme is a swappable
  skin. Establishing an original identity is both safer and higher-scoring.

## Submission checklist

- App listing link (developers.reddit.com/apps/<app>).
- Public demo post in a test subreddit (<200 members), self-explanatory.
- Detailed README.md (what it is + how to play).
- Optional but worth it: <1 min demo video; public repo; developer feedback
  survey (separate prize).
- If counted as an existing project, document the significant hackathon updates.

## Using Kiro is allowed

AI use is explicitly encouraged; it just must not LOOK AI-generated. Polish and a
unique identity are the mitigations, which is already our plan.
